Genetics Q&A

How many genes one person has?

Each of us has 23 pairs of genes. Autosomes are 22 pairs- they determine inherited features of organism. The last pair (23rd) determines sex- chromosome XX is a female, chromosome XY is a male. The role of sex chromosome was discovered by Nettie Stevens in 1905. The american scienist identified the Y chromosome in a mealworm and proved that sex is determined by presence or absence of Y chromosome.

Dad has cleft chin and mum hasn't. What chin will their child have?

It is almost sure that all their children will have cleft chin. Responsible for that is single dominant gene. Every single feature (ie. hair color, eyes color) is described in human genome, by two genes. One pair of genes (allel) makes feature. If the feature has single dominant gene (allel) it doesn't matter if second of pair is dominant or recesive. The dominant gene will be "displayed".

What does DNA stand for?

DNA- deoxyribonucleic acid, stores biological information. In every DNA particle there are usually two chains which are in shape of well known double helix.
